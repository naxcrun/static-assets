Recruitment
Passionate & Effective Recruiting
As Greek Life evolves at Purdue, our recruitment team must be ready to adapt and approach recruitment in an intentional and effective manner. This is arguably one of the most essential efforts for any fraternity to survive, grow, and improve. This guide builds on our experience as a colony and moves us towards full chapter operations and continuous growth. We must maintain our Catholic identity while also expanding to recruit men of all backgrounds to develop them into servant leaders.

Guide Adapted from Phired Up x IFC: The Winning IFC Recruitment Playbook

1. The Strategy: Open Dynamic Recruitment
The days of static, rule-heavy, weeklong rush processes are a thing of the past. For Phi Kappa Theta, recruitment must be a year-round, relationship-building engine.

 

 

Open: Students deserve access to join when it makes sense for them, with no barriers or delays.

 

Dynamic: Your brothers don't wait; they show up early, stay visible, and build genuine relationships. Everyone is expected to take a role in recruitment.

 

To execute this, Phi Kappa Theta must focus on three core priorities:

Priority A: Fill the Funnel
You need to meet way more students, not just the ones who already know they want to go Greek.

 

 

Research shows that 75% of non-affiliated men with interest in fraternities had no idea what to do or who to talk to.

 

Ensure that every student has a genuine connection to a fraternity member.

 

Purdue Tactics: Encourage brothers to volunteer at BGR or NSO. Maintain a key visibility at St. Tom's. Collaborate with them to promote events while creating a referral program to recruit on campus.
 

 

Recruit Leader-Ready PNMs by creating recruitment scholarship and hosting High-Impact Events.

Priority B: Tell the Story
If all guys hear is a cheesy sales pitch, they will tune you out. Students want real connection—something personal, simple, and authentic.

 

 

Show what it's actually like to be a Phi Kap by snapping pictures of intramurals, study nights, or casual hangs.

 

Capture stories of brothers helping each other move in or celebrating a win.

 

Help your brothers swap out buzzwords for real statements like, "This made a big school feel small," or "I didn't know I needed this until I had it".
 

Priority C: Be the Guide
For many students, joining a fraternity can feel confusing or intimidating. Your chapter needs to act as a guide, not a gatekeeper.

 

 

Follow up like a friend using a text, call, or DM to ask, "Need anything?" or "Want help figuring it out?".

 

Share accurate info, answer questions honestly, and care more about the student than the stats.

 


2. The System
A rigid system with registration forms that ask 40 questions or force mandatory fees creates unnecessary friction. Phi Kappa Theta needs a system that connects students with your brothers instantly.

 

 

Make it Simple & Open: Anyone should be able to express interest anytime, with no long forms or unnecessary data collection.

 

Use Qualtrics and PhiKAPP Portal: Use Qualtrics to create a single form for interest, track engagement, and follow up via email and text. Maintain shared and accurate database through PhiKAPP Portal.

 

Direct Outreach: Welcome every student the moment they show interest, and share tips on what to expect. Direct personal text outreach can increase the number of men joining by 25% to 40%.
 

Connected Structure: Work with other PhiKap Leaders at B10 Universities for strategies. Collaborate with alumni for legacy and lasting strategies. Work locally with IFC and FSCL resources to continually update and refine plan to changing culture.
 


3. The Purdue Calendar
Use Purdue's academic rhythm to your advantage. Frame the university's formal "Rush Week" as a launching pad—not a finish line.

 

 

Summer Ramp-Up (May - August): Lay the foundation early when attention is low but access is high. Outreach through social media and scholarship recruitment. Do pre-arrival outreach and introduce yourselves as guides before they even get to West Lafayette. Ensure compliance with IFC Recruitment Rules during BGR.

 

Launch Weeks (August - September): Host High-Impact Rush Events, events involving the St. Tom's Community (SND), and Info Nights.

 

Keep It Going (September - October): Sustain momentum by running a second round of cold outreach via texts to guys who haven't joined yet, letting them know "It's not too late!".

 

Spring Launch (January - February): Push back on the myth that no one joins in the spring. Treat it as a huge opportunity and lead with energy, not excuses. This is especially true within the Catholic community at Purdue in which many may get more involved.

 

Spring Follow-Through (March - May): Keep connecting, host events, and offer another path in.
 


Effective & Passionate Recruitment
Following our call to be Effective & Passionate servant leaders we can apply this to our Recruitment Strategies through three distinctive yet cohesive strategies.

Pitching to Catholic PNMs
For these guys, your chapter is the answer to the challenge of maintaining and growing their faith on a massive secular campus. You offer something incredibly rare: a brotherhood where faith and normal college life seamlessly coexist.

Anchor to the Local Community: Actively highlight the chapter's involvement with the St. Thomas Aquinas Catholic Center. Show them that joining the fraternity immediately plugs them into the broader Catholic community at Purdue.

Showcase the Infrastructure: Don't just tell them you care about faith; prove it. Walk them through the multi-phase spiritual development program and the Bible study your chapter runs. Let them see that there is a structured, intentional path for them to grow spiritually alongside their brothers.

Frame it as a Brotherhood of Iron: College will test their values. Pitch the chapter as a group of guys who will hold them accountable to going to Mass, living with integrity, and navigating the college environment without compromising who they are.

The "Servant Leader" Hook: Connect the mission directly to Christ's example of washing the disciples' feet. It's about leading by putting others first.
The “F Word”

Many Catholic students may be offput by the word Fraternity as it can be associated with a party culture that may not translate to Catholic life. However, we understand and know that Fraternity and Catholic Faith can coincide. Emphasize the word “Brotherhood” over “Fraternity”. Explain that many Newman Centers including St. Tom's originated from fraternities like Phi Kappa Theta and greatly predate them. Live and show our values rooted in Catholic faith.

 

Pitching to Christian (Non-Catholic) PNMs
With Christian PNMs, the focus shifts slightly to shared, universal Christian values. They need to know they will be welcomed, respected, and challenged to grow, even if they don't cross themselves or attend Mass.

Focus on Shared Values: Lean heavily into the "servant leader" aspect. Every Christian understands that true leadership is service. Frame the fraternity as a place where they can live out that calling actively.

Highlight the Bible Study: Emphasize that the chapter's spiritual development program and Bible studies are rooted in Scripture and designed to build better men. This shows that the brotherhood values the Word and active spiritual growth, which resonates across denominations.

Emphasize Accountability: Talk about the culture of the house. Christian PNMs are often looking for a solid group of guys who won't let them slip into the typical campus party-culture pitfalls. Pitch the house as a support system that sharpens them.

Be Upfront but Welcoming: Acknowledge the Catholic roots clearly, but immediately follow up with how that translates into a culture of faith that welcomes any guy striving to live a Christ-centered life.

Pitching to Nonreligious PNMs
For guys who aren't coming from a faith background, "servant leadership" translates directly to character, community impact, and selflessness. You don't need to hide the religious roots, but you should focus the pitch on the tangible, practical outcomes of the mission.

Translate the Mission: Explain that "servant leadership" simply means leading from the front by doing the hard work others won't. It's about ego-free leadership, taking care of your brothers, and serving the Purdue community.

Focus on the "Effective" Part: Highlight the drive for excellence. Talk about how the chapter builds guys who don't just talk about making a difference but actually execute. 

Pitch the Personal Growth: Frame the fraternity as an incubator for becoming a high-value man. It's about graduating not just with a degree, but with the moral character, discipline, and leadership skills required to succeed in their careers and future families.

Define the Brotherhood: Let them know that the shared values of the house create a deeper level of trust. When a group of guys is committed to serving each other rather than themselves, the brotherhood goes way beyond surface-level friendships.

Leadership Day One: As a growing and new fraternity, members can take up leadership positions preparing them for industry and jobs. Emphasize leadership development and the chance to build something new and shape it.

Alumni Connection: Having one of the largest alumni networks at Purdue, emphasize their involvement and support. Highlight how these connections have shaped your career and helped push you forward.

 


Identifying Leaders Framework: FACT
Faithful: Does the potential member demonstrate a strong moral foundation or commitment to faith? 

Available: Is he able and willing to commit time and energy to the fraternity? 

Contagious: Does he bring positive energy and naturally attract others? 

Teachable: Is he open to mentorship and personal growth? 

Recruitment should focus on men who will strengthen both the culture and the leadership of the chapter.
